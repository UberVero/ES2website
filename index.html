FAQ

Do you replace our agency or internal team?

Yes. We believe AI enables modern B2B companies to have a one-person marketing team that orchestrates specialized AI agents using business knowledge only a human can have. Initially, we work alongside the in-house team or agency, and then transfer knowledge of the new agentic system to increase their productivity.

What tools do you work with?

Typically: Notion, GA4, HubSpot/Salesforce, Google and Meta ad platforms, your CMS, and orchestration/automation platforms. We adapt to your stack and will recommend changes only when it materially improves speed or measurement.

How is this different from a traditional marketing agency?

Agencies often execute in one channel at a time. We rebuild the operating system across funnel + measurement, then ship durable workflows your team can run.

How is this different from hiring a full-time channel marketer?

A channel marketer is by definition a specialist who works in a silo. Given modern platforms have API and CLI tools to directly interact with agents, specialists are becoming button-pushers adding little value. We infuse agents with specialist knowledge, enabling you to be the big-picture strategist, freed from executing.

How is this different from a rev ops consultant or fractional CMO?

Consultants and fractional roles might suggest good strategy, but they are not responsible for execution. These people are also rarely technical enough to understand what is actually possible with AI and will always overestimate impact.

What does pricing look like?

We're transparent about pricing. The 60-day Marketing Foundation sprint starts at $8,000. Agent packages (2–3 custom agents with setup and training) typically range $5,000–$15,000. Growth Systems engagements are scoped individually based on channels and complexity. We'll give you a clear number on the first call — no surprises.

How do we start?

We begin with a short discovery to confirm goals, data access, and a scoped plan for the first 4 weeks. Schedule a call to get started.
