After five years of running a Webflow agency, I got tired of juggling six different tools just to manage client projects. So I built something better — and now it's available on the Notion marketplace.


## The Problem: Too Many Tools, Not Enough Flow


Like most consultancies, Eldur Studio was running on a fragile stack: Linear for tasks, Pipedrive for deals, Xero for invoices, Gamma for proposals, Slack for updates, and Notion for everything else.


The result? Constant context switching, broken integrations, and clients asking "where do I find that again?"


I needed one system that could handle the full lifecycle of a Webflow project — from first contact to final invoice — without requiring clients to log into three platforms or me to remember which Zapier connection broke this week.

## What I Built


I consolidated everything into Notion and started automating the repetitive stuff. Used it on real client work. Refined it through dozens of projects. When other Webflow designers saw it, they wanted it too.


This isn't a generic project management template with a Webflow logo on it. It's purpose-built for how Webflow professionals actually work:

- **Full business dashboard.** Track your sales pipeline and revenue in one view. See which projects are profitable, forecast monthly income, and manage contacts — no separate CRM needed.
- **Webflow-native project workflow.** Pre-filled tasks that reflect actual Webflow best practices, from design handoff to CMS setup to pre-launch QA. Clients get a clean view of progress. You keep internal tasks separate.
- **Invoicing without the headaches.** Invoice tracking with automated reminders, all native to Notion. Connect your email and Slack, and you're done. No third-party integrations that break.
- **Automations that do the boring work.** Scoping helper that generates proposals from your notes. Invoice prep that drafts line items from completed tasks. Weekly client updates compiled automatically. Risk flags before blocked work becomes a problem.
