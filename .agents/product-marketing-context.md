## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Custom AI agents for B2B growth — marketing and sales ops work, built and handed off.
**What it does:** Eldur Studio audits the client's funnel, builds AI agents for lead qualification, content publishing, ad monitoring, and CRM hygiene, wires them into the existing stack, then hands over systems the team can run independently. No ongoing dependency.
**Product category:** Outsourced marketing + rev ops (operator-led) with AI agent orchestration
**Product type:** Custom development service, project-based
**Business model:** Fixed-price project delivery in three tiers:
- **First Agent** — $12k–$25k, 4–6 weeks. 1 orchestrator + 4–6 skills for one job.
- **Agent System** — $25k–$55k, 6–8 weeks. 2–3 orchestrators + cross-agent reporting + funnel/tracking fixes.
- **Marketing Bot Factory** — $12k–$20k/month, 3–6 months. Full-stack orchestration replacing headcount or agency retainer.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** B2B SaaS, health tech, biotech. Technical buyers or regulated/high-trust contexts where proof and measurement matter.
**Stage:** Post-PMF. Typically Series A–B or bootstrapped with ~$2M+ ARR. Not ready for a full marketing org, but marketing can't be a side project.
**Budget signal:** ~$10k–$25k/month equivalent spend. Often already paying this across agencies or headcount.
**Decision-makers:** Founders/CEOs, revenue/growth/marketing operators, RevOps and sales ops leads
**Primary use case:** Replace manual, repeatable marketing and sales ops work with autonomous AI agents that the client team owns and runs
**Jobs to be done:**
- Predictable qualified pipeline without building a department
- Multi-channel execution and reporting without a bloated stack
- Clean CRM and better lead routing; fewer unqualified leads reaching sales
- Systems the company keeps and can run without ongoing vendor dependency
**Use cases:**
- Intake + qualification agent (classifies every inbound lead with reason codes, routes to right owner)
- Content pipeline agent (keyword research → draft in client voice → SEO check → publish)
- SEM monitoring agent (daily spend/CPA/conversion monitoring, weekly change log)
- CRM hygiene agent (meeting auto-logging, deduplication, Monday follow-up prioritization)
- Profitability agent (connects ad spend → pipeline → ROAS/snapshot for exec review)
- Relationship mapping

**Disqualifiers (anti-persona):** Pharma compliance-heavy work, brand-only engagements, pre-PMF companies (unless tightly scoped validation sprint), companies wanting ongoing managed service rather than handoff.

## Industry Problems Eldur Studio Solves
*(Frame these as buyer-recognizable symptoms)*

- **AI adoption trap:** Teams buy tools and test prompts but don't get reliable workflows or measurable outcomes.
- **Accountability gap:** Strategy and execution are split across agencies, freelancers, and internal handoffs — nobody owns outcomes.
- **Measurement chaos:** Data exists but is fragmented. Even basic questions are hard to answer: what drives qualified pipeline, what converts, what is profitable.
- **Agency economics are misaligned:** Clients pay for overhead and process, not results. AI efficiencies rarely show up in cost or speed.

**Pains → Needs mapping:**

| Pain | Need |
|------|------|
| AI adds complexity, not results | A single accountable owner who ships |
| Too many handoffs and vendors | A measurable operating system (tracking + reporting) |
| Unclear attribution and pipeline impact | Repeatable growth workflows that don't require heroic effort |
| Execution slows down as the stack grows | Durable assets the company can run independently |

## Brand Voice

**Voice identity:** Professional, direct, and action-oriented with a warm, personal touch. A smart colleague who gets to the point, genuinely cares about your success, and never hides behind jargon.

**Four voice attributes:**
1. **Professional yet personal** — warm and relationship-driven; maintains credibility without being stiff or corporate
2. **Clear and concise** — short paragraphs, bullet points, direct; respectful of people's time; precision, not oversimplification
3. **Action-oriented** — drives results through clear asks, specific next steps, concrete deliverables; not pushy or urgency-manufactured
4. **Authentic and transparent** — honest about challenges, enthusiastic about wins; shows the human behind the work without oversharing

**Core messaging pillars (priority order):**
1. Accountability — single owner, not fragmented agencies. "We embed with your team."
2. Measurement first — decisions based on data, not guesswork. "Fix the funnel first."
3. Durable systems — build things teams can run independently. "Durable systems your team can run."
4. AI as orchestration — AI agents + human strategy, not tool replacement.
5. Speed with substance — 60-day sprints, clear milestones, fast shipping — never rushing past the fundamentals.

**Tone by channel:**
| Channel | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Blog / articles | Informative, conversational, problem-first structure |
| LinkedIn | Professional, thought-provoking, concise — no empty inspiration |
| Client emails | Warm, direct, action-oriented. Use first names. |
| Cold outreach | Professional, specific, value-forward. No exclamation points. |
| Sales / service pages | Confident, specific, benefit-driven with proof |

**Style rules:**
- Oxford comma: yes
- Headings: sentence case
- Contractions: use them (we're, don't, it's)
- Em dashes: with spaces — like this
- Numbers: spell out 1–9, numerals 10+
- Percent: use % symbol
- Exclamation points: genuine excitement only, max 1 per piece (friendly emails excepted)
- Emoji: 1–2 max, purposeful. 🎉 for launches, 🙏 for asks. Never decorative strings.
- Brand name: always "Eldur Studio" — never just "Eldur"

**AI writing patterns to avoid:**
- "Not X. It's Y." reframes (LinkedIn hook formula — replace with a real observation)
- Numbered listicle intros ("Three things made X the right fit")
- Overly structured declarations ("We designed X around four components, each with one job")
- Abstract contrast formulas ("This is the difference between X and Y")
- Generic results wrap-ups ("The CRM stopped being something the team caught up on")
- Impersonal CTAs ("X is one of the highest-leverage things you can build")

**The litmus test:** Read each paragraph aloud. If it could have been written about any company by any author, it needs more specificity — a real detail, an honest aside, a named experience.
